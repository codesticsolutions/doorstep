Frequently Asked Questions About Home Health Care

What is the difference between a home nurse and a home health attendant?

A nurse provides medical treatments, injections, wound care, and clinical monitoring. A home health attendant assists with daily living activities (bathing, feeding, mobility), medication reminders, and basic health checks under professional supervision. We provide both services.

Are your home health attendants trained and certified?

Yes, all our attendants are certified in Basic Life Support, medication administration, and patient care standards. They undergo rigorous background checks and continuous training in elderly care, dementia support, and post-surgery rehabilitation.

Do you provide 24/7 home nursing services in Karachi?

Absolutely. We offer round-the-clock nursing care with attendants available for day, night, or 24-hour shifts. Whether you need live-in care or part-time assistance, we can arrange qualified staff 24/7.

Can you provide a medical attendant for post-surgery care at home?

Yes, we specialize in post-operative home care. Our attendants assist with wound care, medication schedules, mobility support, and monitor vital signs to ensure a smooth recovery after surgery.
